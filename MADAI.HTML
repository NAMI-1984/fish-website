マダイ (Madai)

Red Sea Bream

Scientific Classification

Order: Perciformes

Family: Sparidae

Scientific Name: Pagrus major

English Name: Red Sea Bream

About Madai

Madai, or Red Sea Bream, is one of the most celebrated fish in Japanese cuisine. Known for its delicate flavor and firm texture, it is often served as sashimi, grilled, or in traditional dishes like "Tai Meshi" (sea bream rice).

Seasonality

Madai is best enjoyed during spring, which is its spawning season and when its flavor is most balanced.

Did You Know?

Madai is a symbol of good luck and prosperity in Japan, often served at celebrations such as weddings and New Year festivities.

Fun Fact

The vibrant red color of Madai makes it a standout in Japanese markets and a favorite choice for decorative food presentations.
