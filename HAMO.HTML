ハモ (Hamo)

Pike Conger

Scientific Classification

Order: Anguilliformes

Family: Congridae

Scientific Name: Muraenesox cinereus

English Name: Pike Conger

About Hamo

Hamo, or Pike Conger, is a highly valued fish in Japanese cuisine, particularly in the Kansai region. Known for its delicate flavor and smooth texture, it is often prepared as tempura, shabu-shabu, or grilled dishes.

Seasonality

Hamo is best enjoyed during the summer months when it is considered a refreshing delicacy to combat the heat.

Did You Know?

The intricate bone-removal process called "hone-kiri" is a traditional skill used to prepare Hamo, ensuring a perfect dining experience without compromising its delicate flesh.

Fun Fact

Hamo is not only prized for its taste but also its resilience. It is said that Hamo can survive out of water for extended periods, symbolizing strength and endurance in Japanese culture.
