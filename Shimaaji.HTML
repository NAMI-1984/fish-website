シマアジ (Shimaaji)

Stripe Jack

About Shimaaji

Shimaaji, also known as Stripe Jack, is a prized fish in Japanese cuisine. It is known for its light, delicate flavor and smooth texture, making it a popular choice for sashimi and sushi. Its name "Shimaaji" translates to "striped horse mackerel," referring to the distinct stripes on its body.

Seasonality

Shimaaji is best enjoyed in the summer months, when its flavor is at its peak.

Did You Know?

Shimaaji is often farmed to ensure consistent quality and availability, but wild-caught Shimaaji is highly sought after for its superior taste.
