寒ぶり (Kanburi)

Japanese Amberjack

Scientific Classification

Order: Perciformes

Family: Carangidae

Scientific Name: Seriola quinqueradiata

English Name: Japanese Amberjack

About Kanburi

Kanburi, or Japanese Amberjack, is a highly prized fish in Japan, especially during the winter months when its fat content is at its peak. Known for its rich flavor and tender texture, it is a favorite in traditional dishes such as sashimi and teriyaki.

Seasonality

Kanburi is best enjoyed in winter, and its quality is particularly celebrated in regions like Toyama Prefecture, home to the renowned "Himi Buri."

Himi Buri

Himi Buri refers to the highest-quality Kanburi caught in the cold waters off Himi, Toyama. This variety is known for its exceptional fat content and is considered a premium delicacy. At our restaurant, we are proud to use authentic Himi Buri to deliver the best flavors to our customers.

Did You Know?

The word "Kanburi" literally translates to "winter yellowtail," reflecting its seasonality and premium quality during colder months.

Fun Fact

Kanburi can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, making it one of the largest fish commonly used in Japanese cuisine. Its lifecycle includes different names at various stages, such as "Inada" and "Warasa," before reaching maturity as "Buri."
