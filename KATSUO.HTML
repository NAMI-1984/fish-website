カツオ (Katsuo)

Skipjack Tuna

Scientific Classification

Order: Perciformes

Family: Scombridae

Scientific Name: Katsuwonus pelamis

English Name: Skipjack Tuna

About Katsuo

Katsuo, or Skipjack Tuna, is a staple in Japanese cuisine, particularly famous for its use in making katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes) and tataki (seared sashimi). Its rich umami flavor and firm texture make it a beloved choice for a variety of dishes.

Seasonality

Katsuo is best enjoyed in spring and autumn, known as "Hatsu-gatsuo" (first bonito) and "Modori-gatsuo" (returning bonito) respectively, when it is at its peak flavor.

Did You Know?

Katsuo has been a significant part of Japanese culinary history for centuries, and katsuobushi is considered an essential ingredient in traditional dashi soup stock.

Fun Fact

The streamlined body of Katsuo allows it to swim at high speeds, making it a challenging and exciting catch for fishermen.
