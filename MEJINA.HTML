メジナ (Mejina)

Opaleye

Scientific Classification

Order: Perciformes

Family: Girellidae

Scientific Name: Girella punctata

English Name: Opaleye

About Mejina

Mejina, or Opaleye, is a popular fish in Japanese cuisine, known for its firm, white flesh and slightly sweet flavor. It is often grilled or simmered in soy-based sauces, highlighting its natural taste.

Seasonality

Mejina is best enjoyed in winter, when the fish is at its peak flavor and fat content.

Did You Know?

Mejina is known for its adaptability, thriving in both rocky coastal areas and reefs, where it feeds on algae and small invertebrates.

Fun Fact

The name "Mejina" comes from its shiny, reflective scales, which are said to resemble polished gemstones.
