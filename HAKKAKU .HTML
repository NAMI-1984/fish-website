ハッカク (Hakkaku)

Sailfin Poacher

Scientific Classification

Order: Scorpaeniformes

Family: Agonidae

Scientific Name: Podothecus sachi

English Name: Sailfin Poacher

About Hakkaku

Hakkaku, or Sailfin Poacher, is a rare and unique fish found in the cold waters of the North Pacific. Known for its firm texture and delicate flavor, it is a prized ingredient in Japanese cuisine, often prepared grilled or simmered.

Seasonality

Hakkaku is best enjoyed during winter, when its flavor is at its peak, offering a rich and savory experience.

Did You Know?

The name "Hakkaku" refers to its eight distinct fins, a characteristic feature that distinguishes it from other fish. Its armor-like scales provide protection and contribute to its unique appearance.

Fun Fact

Hakkaku is often served in high-end Japanese restaurants, where its distinctive look and exquisite flavor are showcased in traditional dishes.
